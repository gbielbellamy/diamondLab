Engineering Microbiomes Symposium at UCSF — February 2026

Event

Carlos attended the Engineering Microbiomes Symposium held on February 3, 2026, at the UCSF Parnassus Campus. During the meeting, he presented a poster highlighting his work on developing in vitro microbiome "communoids" as an experimental platform for microbial target discovery and microbiome editing, where he aims to create controlled experimental systems to better understand microbial interactions and enable precise manipulation of microbiome communities. The symposium provided an opportunity to share these approaches with leading researchers in the field.

International Symposium on Plasmid Biology, 2024

International Event

Spencer and Rebecca attended the International Symposium on Plasmid Biology in Hamamatsu, Japan, narrowly dodging Typhoon Shanshan. Rebecca presented a poster on our cow rumen plasmidome research, generating a lot of interest in our metagenomic Hi-C approach to link plasmids and hosts. We left Japan with new friends in the plasmid research community and a profound respect for Japanese toilet technology.
