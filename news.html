Engineering Microbiomes Symposium at UCSF- February 2026
Event

Carlos attended the Engineering Microbiomes Symposium held on February 3, 2026, at the UCSF Parnassus Campus. During the meeting, he presented a poster highlighting his work on developing in vitro microbiome “communoids” as an experimental platform for microbial target discovery and microbiome editing, where he aims to create controlled experimental systems to better understand microbial interactions and enable precise manipulation of microbiome communities. The symposium provided an opportunity to share these approaches with leading researchers in the field.

Highlights from Inside IGI Climate 2024
Lab News

Our lab was pleased to participate in the Inside IGI Climate 2024 Conference, held at the Pauley Ballroom on the UC Berkeley campus. The event brought together researchers from across and outside IGI to share their work and discuss solutions to pressing climate challenges.

Spencer delivered a talk about our ongoing research on the rumen microbiome and its role in methane emission mitigation. It was a great opportunity to share the progress and focus of our research with others in the community. During the poster session, which featured research projects from across IGI, our lab was also recognized for its contributions. Congratulations to our postdocs Rebecca and Carlos, who tied for 3rd place in their poster presentations!

International Symposium on Plasmid Biology, 2024
International Event

Spencer and Rebecca attended the International Symposium on Plasmid Biology in Hamamatsu, Japan, narrowly dodging Typhoon Shanshan. Engaging with cutting-edge plasmid research kept us focused as we overcame the 17-hour jetlag and 24-hour karaoke. Rebecca presented a poster on our cow rumen plasmidome research, generating a lot of interest in our metagenomic Hi-C approach to link plasmids and hosts. We left Japan with new friends in the plasmid research community and a profound respect for Japanese toilet technology.

Representing IGI at SACNAS NDiSTEM 2024
Event

Carlos, a postdoctoral researcher at our lab, had the chance to be part of the team representing IGI at the SACNAS NDiSTEM 2024 Conference. This year marked a special milestone for IGI as the institute joined the event for the first time with an exhibitor booth. The team had the chance to connect with attendees, sharing insights about the impactful research happening at IGI, as well as opportunities to get involved.

For Carlos, attending SACNAS was an opportunity to inspire the next generation of scientists by discussing the research being conducted in our lab, while also networking with other fellow scientists. By sharing his journey, he wanted to motivate young researchers, encouraging them to explore careers in STEM and emphasizing the importance of diversity in science.
